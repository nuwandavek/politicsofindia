2. MPs [2014 Lok Sabha]

I realized I had begun the story, without context. So, for the super-beings observing this simulation, here goes. I'll be working with the dataset of MPs of the current Lok Sabha for the next few weeks, trying to answer questions that interest me.

An Overview

HELLO

This is India, my homeland. It is divided into 543 constituencies (as of 2017 Sep.) and each unit sends a representative to the Lok Sabha, to ensure its interests are being served while laws and policies are being framed.

RESERVATION

The constitution of India reserves some constituencies for SCs(15.4% of Constituencies) and STs(8.6% of Constituencies). This was done to empower the disenfranchized, and further democratize the agency of governance.

PARTIES - LS MPS

In the 2014 Lok Sabha elections, Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) won a staggering 51.93% of seats, followed by Indian National Congress (INC) at a distant 8.1% seats. The BJP, along with its allies in the National Democratic Alliance (NDA), formed the central government of India.
Southern and Eastern India seem to resist the sway of the BJP, at least in this election.

GENDER - LS MPS

For a nation with over 500 million women, a dismal 11.4% of seats were won by them.

EDUCATION - LS MPS

74.77% of the winners have studied well beyond 12th Standard with 33.7% post-graduates and doctorates. There are 69% women winners over 12STD+ compared to 75.5%, among men winners.

CRIMINAL CASES - LS MPS

34.3% of winners have criminal cases against them with 3% having >=10 criminal cases against them. 64% male winners have zero criminal cases against them whereas the number of female winners without criminal cases stands at 74%

MONEY - LS MPS

This Lok Sabha has 81.76% Crorepatis (what's the gender neutral term for this?) ranging from a net worth of 34k to 683 crores and a median of 3.25 crores.
